The University of Houston (UH) is a public research university in Houston, 
Texas and the main institution of the University of Houston System.[6]
 Founded in 1927, UH is the third-largest university in Texas with over 46,000 students.[7] 
Its campus spans 667 acres in southeast Houston, and was known as University of Houston
 Uiversity Park from 1983 to 1991.[8][9] 
The Carnegie Foundation classifies UH as a doctoral degree-granting institution
 with "highest research activity."[10][11][12]

The University of Houston began as Houston Junior College (HJC). 
On March 7, 1927, trustees of the Houston Independent School District (HISD) 
Board of Education unanimously passed a 
resolution that authorized the founding and operating of a junior college. 
The junior college was operated and administered by HISD
